Parippu Vada

Parippu Vada is a traditional evening snack of Kerala

Parippu Vada is a form of fritters made with lentils, usually chana dal (Bengal Gram or Split Chickpea Lentils) and toor dal(Split Pigeon Peas). These lentils are first soaked for a few hours, ground coarsely, and then mixed with finely chopped onions, ginger, green chilies, and curry leaves. Small balls are made and flattened which are then deep-fried in oil.

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup Chana dal
  • ½ cup Toor dal
  • 10-12 Shallots
  • 2-3 Green chilies
  • 3-4 Dry Red chilies
  • 1 tbsp Ginger
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri Red chili powder (optional)
  • 2-3 sprigs of Curry Leaves
  • Coconut oil, to fry
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak the lentils for a couple of hours.
  2. Wash and strain and keep aside for another 30 minutes.
  3. Finely chop and combine shallots, green chilies, dry red chilies, ginger, and curry leaves.
  4. Coarsely grind the dal and mix it with the shallot mixture
  5. Heat oil for frying.
  6. Add salt (and Kashmiri Chili powder) to the dal mixture and combine again.
  7. Take a lemon-sized portion of it and flatten between your palms. Press the sides with your fingers to get the desired shape of Parippu Vada which is slightly bulgy at the center.
  8. Fry in hot oil until golden brown
  9. Remove and serve warm with evening tea.
